Unveiling the Computational Principles of Intelligence

My research interests primarily revolve around foundational AI for robust, interpretable, and safe intelligence. I investigate how high-level abstractions arise in deep models and how such representations might reveal the computational principles underlying intelligence. My work spans the intersection of deep learning theory, causal representational learning, multimodal intelligence, interpretability & explainable AI, and AI safety & alignment.

Key Research Areas

Foundational AI for Robust & Safe Intelligence

Investigating the core principles and architectures that lead to AI systems that are not only powerful but also reliable, secure, and resilient against adversarial attacks or unexpected failures. This forms the bedrock of building trustworthy AI.

Deep Learning Theory

Delving into the theoretical underpinnings of deep neural networks, including generalization capabilities, optimization landscapes, and the emergence of hierarchical representations.

Causal Representational Learning

Developing methods to identify and disentangle causal factors within data, allowing models to learn more robust and interpretable representations that generalize better to new environments.

Multimodal Intelligence

Exploring how AI can integrate and reason across different data modalities (e.g., vision, language, sound) to achieve a more comprehensive understanding of the world, mimicking human cognitive abilities.

Interpretability & Explainable AI (XAI)

Creating transparent AI systems by developing techniques to understand, visualize, and explain their decision-making processes, thereby fostering trust and enabling critical analysis.

AI Safety & Alignment

Dedicated to ensuring that advanced AI systems are aligned with human values and intentions, preventing unintended consequences and promoting beneficial outcomes for society. This includes scalable oversight and robust ethical frameworks.
